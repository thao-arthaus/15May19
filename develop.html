Develop

in your current role

There’s no shortage of career growth opportunities at HSBC. In fact, every day can present opportunities to develop your skills, help you master your role and achieve your career goals. We want to be here throughout your career journey, helping you to drive your personal and professional development, and to make the most of every opportunity.

Development doesn’t just come from a training course. There are lots of ways you can develop whilst doing your day job and people in our business can help you too. We’ve put together a range of suggestions and tools to give you some ideas about what to do next.

HSBC’s approach to mentoring

And how personalised guidance can support your development

30 min read

What is mentoring?

“Mentoring is to support and encourage people to manage their own learning in order that they may maximise their potential, develop their skills, improve their performance and become the person they want to be.”

Eric Parsloe, Director of The Oxford School of Coaching and Mentoring

Internal vs external mentors

Mentors can be based either within our business or outside of it, and both come with their own advantages. For example, if you’re new to HSBC, an internal mentor can help you navigate our work and office culture. Alternatively, an external mentor can give you an impartial opinion. It all depends on what you’re looking for guidance on – and sometimes it’s helpful to have both.

Some of the benefits of mentoring

Create new connections

You can build professional relationships that support your growth.

Increase self confidence

You might surprise yourself by embracing new roles, responsibilities and challenges.

Gain useful advice

If you’re open to it, you’ll receive guidance that may lead to new responsibilities.

Set the bar higher

Be encouraged to take greater risks, set new goals and achieve at a higher level.

Enjoy increased visibility

Opportunities to network with peers and colleagues and improve your professional profile, can come up.

Find inspiration

You’ll meet role models who’ll help you hone your professional leadership style.

Commit to your own growth

You’ll reinforce your decisions with action plans for personal and professional development.

Improve your abilities

Develop your competencies and build even stronger interpersonal skills.
