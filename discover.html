Our approach to career development at HSBC

Career Development Discover our approach to career development at HSBC

Welcome to HSBC. We know a business of our scale can be complex to navigate when you first join so we want to make it as straightforward for you as possible.

Our aim is to create a workplace where all our people can enjoy healthy, challenging, and rewarding careers. We believe in giving you the freedom to be yourself at work and that includes giving you the opportunities to build a career that’s meaningful to you – whether that means becoming a specialist in one field, or developing across a range of areas.

We want to empower you to work to your full potential, discover opportunities to grow and succeed, and we hope the tools, guidance and resources here will give you a head start.

Your first 90 days at HSBC

And the start of an inspiring career

30 min read

As exciting as a new role in a business like ours is, it can sometimes be daunting too. We’ve found that creating a 90-day plan can help you find your feet and settle in, in your new workplace.

The 90-day plan is also a great starting-point for defining your broader career goals. Once you’ve learnt the basics of your new job and have a sense of the diverse opportunities on offer, start thinking about the skills and knowledge you’ll need to make the most of them. The clearer your ideas of how you’d like to grow, the easier they’ll be to achieve. Chat to you manager too, they can help you draw up a development plan.

Career development tips

And how to start planning your career journey

2 min read

What do you want from your career

Not everybody wants to be CEO, but that doesn’t mean you don’t have ambition. Think about what’s important to you, and what you’d like to learn more about. Knowing your strengths can also be a great place to start.

Our My Strengths tool can help you define these and give you ideas about what to work towards.

How can you grow from everyday experiences?

Professional development isn’t just about attending training courses. Learning should happen every day as part of our job.

Our Develop in Role guide can help you work out your learning goals, as well as ways you can work towards them and gain more experience in your current role.

Is great performance part of your plan?

At HSBC, delivering great performance in your current role is often the first step to building a successful career.

Look for opportunities to demonstrate your strengths and build new skills, knowledge and experience, every day.

Do you want to diversify or specialise?

Some people are interested in developing across a wide variety of career experiences and some focus on becoming an expert in one particular area.

Which do you prefer and are there people who you know have done something similar? What advice can they offer?

Do you actively explore new opportunities?

Think beyond promotions. You can build your skills and experience in your current role, and through sideways and cross-functional career moves.

Try expanding your networks and meeting people who can tell you about different roles. You can also use CareerLink to search for new opportunities.

Make the most of the support that’s on offer!

Do you have a mentor? Do you get constructive feedback and advice through your Everyday Performance and Development discussions with your manager?

Visit Develop in your current role to see all the tools and resources we offer, including HSBC University and HRDirect, to support your development.

Our culture

Get a head start to understand our culture and heritage

2 min read

Our culture

Often we focus so intently on understanding the ‘what’ of a new job, that the ‘how’ can be overlooked. To help you get off to a quick start, here are some things worth knowing about HSBC’s culture.

Our culture influences the interactions we have with our customers and one another every single day. It is rooted in our history as a trading bank in Hong Kong whose purpose is to connect people to opportunities. It is shaped by values that recognise that to do this well, means being dependable, open and connected.

HSBC culture is evolving as we face new opportunities and we have big aspirations for both you and us.

We aspire to have the healthiest human system in the financial industry. That means we should all feel able to bring our whole selves to work in an environment that supports diversity of thought and heritage along with physical and mental well-being.

We all acknowledge the responsibilities each of us have both inside and outside of work and encourage a healthy work/life balance to help our people deliver professionally and personally.

We are focused on building trusted relationships with one another and with our customers.

Leadership in HSBC is about having an ‘exist to serve’ mentality helping colleagues and customers achieve their goals.

We aim to equip our people with the knowledge and resources have a career that meets their aspirations and potential.

We understand the importance and impact of ‘how I show up’ and understand the impact we have on others.

Our values

Get a head start on understanding our values

2 min read

Our values act as fundamental guidelines on how all of us show up at work and interact with colleagues, stakeholders and clients. Understanding and embracing our values will help you quickly understand the culture at HSBC and help you settle in. Representing our values through behavior will support your career progression throughout your time at HSBC.

At HSBC, we believe that how we do business is as important as what we do. We want to achieve good results in a way that treats our customers fairly and helps to strengthen communities and ensure a properly functioning financial system. Our values are central to achieving these aims. HSBC’s values define who we are as an organisation and what makes us distinctive. We believe in acting with courageous integrity. We are:

Dependable

Standing firm for what is right, delivering on commitments, being resilient and trustworthy.

Taking personal accountability, being decisive, using judgment and common sense, empowering others.

Open to different ideas and cultures

Communicating openly, honestly and transparently, welcoming challenge, learning from mistakes.

Listening, treating people fairly, being inclusive, valuing different perspectives.

Connected to customers, communities, regulators and each other

Building connections, being aware of external issues, collaborating across boundaries.

Caring about individuals and their progress, showing respect, being supportive and responsive.

Development planning

And how to set yourself up for success

30 min read

Development planning for your career can help you fulfil your potential and achieve long-term goals, but it can also help you succeed in the short-term. You could even find yourself exceeding expectations.

Try using the using the SMART framework to achieve your goals:

Specify

Set yourself a clear goal that you want to achieve

Measurable

Decide how you’ll measure progress towards your goal

Achievable

Create smaller targets along the way that are feasible and motivating

Relavant

Make sure your goals are relevant to your career path

Timed

Set yourself a deadline
